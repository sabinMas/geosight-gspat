# Demo Scripts

## 90 Seconds

- Open the Columbia River infrastructure story from the landing page.
- Let the mission run load, then show the active recommendation and the mission score together.
- Open one supporting trust surface: either factor evidence or source awareness.
- Point out that the score separates direct live, derived live, and proxy heuristic inputs.
- Generate a GeoScribe report or mention that the same live context can become a structured site assessment.
- Close with the line: GeoSight is a spatial reasoning system with provenance, not a generic map or chatbot.

## 3 Minutes

- Start with the Columbia River story and explain the user question: which candidate looks strongest for cooling-intensive infrastructure?
- Show the mission run, score, and comparison card together.
- Open source awareness briefly to prove the recommendation is grounded in real providers and explicit confidence language.
- Surface one of the new subsurface cards such as groundwater or soil profile to show that GeoSight now looks beneath the surface, not only at visible map context.
- Switch to Tokyo to prove the same workflow still works globally and to show that GeoSight names limited coverage honestly instead of bluffing.
- End by generating a GeoScribe report or summarizing that the same evidence bundle becomes a reusable written deliverable.

## 5 Minutes

- Open with Columbia River and keep the first minute focused on the mission objective, recommendation, and why the top candidate wins.
- Open factor breakdown and highlight the evidence-kind labels and methodology note pattern.
- Open one or two supporting cards that feel memorable: cooling water, groundwater, soil profile, seismic design, or climate history.
- Open source awareness and show regional provider logic, freshness, confidence, and fallback-provider language.
- Switch to Tokyo and explain the difference between global direct live signals and weaker proxy or national-scale context.
- Switch to Bellevue, Washington and show school context plus one residential risk or subsurface card.
- Generate a GeoScribe report to show the product can move from exploration to a shareable artifact.
- Close by explaining the fallback story: even when a provider is slow, GeoSight keeps the trust model visible and can fall back to deterministic evidence or captured demo assets.

## Presenter Notes

- Do not explain every card.
- Stop on the first strong recommendation and justify it with evidence.
- Use trust language sparingly but clearly: provider, freshness, confidence, and evidence kind.
- Keep the demo moving from place to place, not from menu to menu.
- If the fallback banner appears, treat it as proof that the app is honest about live-loading conditions.
